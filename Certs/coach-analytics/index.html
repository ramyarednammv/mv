How This Affects Performance Numbers

When a campaign is sent, the multi-list leads (~234K) receive it on their primary list (Daily, Members, etc.) and are suppressed from the Coach send. What remains is the Coach-only segment (~211K) — people who are only on the Coach list. This residual audience is inherently less engaged because they haven't opted into a higher-engagement list. All Coach OR numbers in this report should be read with this context: most metrics reflect the Coach-only segment, not the full 434K list.

Section 03

The Suppression Effect — Why Coach OR Looks Low

The Coach list sits at the bottom of the send hierarchy. When a campaign goes out, it's sent first to Daily (916K), Members (212K), Highlights (403K), and Weekly (335K). Anyone on those lists is suppressed from the Coach send. What remains is the residual audience — leads who are only on the Coach list and no other.

Section 14

Strategic Insights & Recommendations

1

The Coach List Isn't Underperforming — It's Over-Suppressed

The reported 16.5% average OR is misleading. When sent unsuppressed, the Coach list achieves 25-31% OR. The "low performance" narrative is an artifact of the send hierarchy: Coach is always last, and the most engaged leads (multi-list subscribers) are already suppressed. Any benchmarking must account for suppression levels.

2

Vishen's Newsletter Is the True Engagement Anchor

With 52% average suppression, Vishen's Newsletter achieving 17.6% OR on the residual audience is the strongest suppression-adjusted performance of any recurring content. It also has the best CTR (0.27%) and CTOR (~1.5%). This send keeps the Coach-only segment warm week after week.

3

CLC Model = The Playbook

CLC campaigns only suppress CLC-specific lists (not broadcast lists), reaching 90%+ of the Coach list. Result: consistent 20-25% OR. This should be the model for all Coach-list campaigns — suppress event-specific lists only, not broadcast audiences. The Coach list was built for coaching content; serve it directly.

4

Address List Decay

136K subscribers lost (-23.9%) since April 2025. At the current rate, the list drops below 400K by mid-2026. The steepest drops coincide with high-suppression, high-volume months. Recommend: (1) audit whether "unsubscribes" are true churn or list hygiene, (2) add new Coach acquisition flows, (3) consider a re-engagement campaign for dormant Coach-only leads.

5

Subject Lines That Work on This Audience

Winners: Curiosity gaps ("I followed every rule. But..." — 23.6%), urgency ("free pass expiring" — 25.1%), identity triggers ("You're Meant to be a Life Coach" — 26.2%). Losers: Income claims ("She made $10,000" — 12.2%), generic urgency ("[12 hours left]" — 10.5%). The Coach list resists hype but responds to professional development framing.

6

Reduce Non-Coaching Campaign Volume

91% of Coach sends are invite sequences for events that aren't coaching-specific — and most are heavily suppressed, yielding 12-15% OR on the residual audience. These sends add volume without much impact and may accelerate list fatigue. Consider whether generic summit invites need to hit the Coach list at all, or whether the suppressed residual segment provides meaningful incremental reach.
