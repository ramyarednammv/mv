Why This Matters

When a campaign goes out, the 227K multi-list leads receive it on their primary list first (Daily, Members, etc.) and are suppressed from the Coach send. The Coach send only reaches the remaining ~211K "Coach-only" leads. This is why Coach open rates appear lower — they mostly measure the least engaged segment of the list.

02

How Coach Emails Actually Get Sent

Mindvalley sends campaigns to lists in a priority order. Each list suppresses people who already received the email on a higher-priority list:

1. Daily — 916K — Sent first, full audience
2. Members — 212K — Sent early, mostly full
3. Highlights — 403K — Some suppression
4. Weekly — 335K — Some suppression
5. Coach — 438K total, but only ~211K reached after suppression
The Exception: Coach-Specific Campaigns

Some campaigns — like CLC launches and Modern Coach Challenge — only suppress event-specific lists (e.g., seminar signups), not the broadcast lists. These reach 90-97% of the full Coach list and give us the true picture of Coach engagement: 20-25% OR.

10

Recommendations

1

The Coach List Isn't Broken — The Measurement Is

When the full list is reached (CLC, MCH), OR is 20-25%. When only the residual is reached (most other campaigns), OR is 12-16%. Any benchmarking of Coach performance must account for which audience actually received the email.

2

Vishen's Newsletter Is the Engagement Anchor

17.6% OR and 0.27% CTR on the hardest segment of the list (52% suppressed, Coach-only leads) makes Vishen's NL the strongest suppression-adjusted performer. It keeps the Coach-only segment warm between campaigns.

3

Use the CLC Suppression Model

CLC only suppresses event-specific lists, not broadcast lists. Result: 90-97% of Coach reached, 20-25% OR. Apply this model to future coaching campaigns — suppress the sign-up list, not Daily/Members/Highlights.

4

Address the 136K List Decline

From 570K peak to 434K (-23.9%). Steepest drops coincided with high-volume residual months. Recommended: re-engagement campaign for dormant Coach-only leads, sunset policy for 90-day inactives, new acquisition flows.

5

Subject Lines: Identity > Hype

Winners: curiosity gaps ("I followed every rule. But..." 23.6%), identity triggers ("You're Meant to be a Life Coach" 26.2%), urgency ("free pass expiring" 25.1%). Losers: income claims ("She made $10,000" 12.2%). This audience responds to professional growth, not hype.

6

Question Residual-Only Sends

Is it worth sending AI Summit, Entrepreneurship Summit, and Spiritual Summit invites to the residual Coach segment at 12-14% OR? These add volume (25+ emails/month in peak periods) but may accelerate fatigue on the Coach-only leads. Consider reducing non-coaching sends to the residual.
