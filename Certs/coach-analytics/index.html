Section 12

Strategic Insights & Recommendations

1

Stop Treating Coach as a Broadcast List

91% of Coach emails are invite sequences for events that aren't coaching-specific. The list's best performance comes from CLC launches (21-26% OR) and coaching newsletters (20.6% OR). Generic summits drag the average down to 12-14% OR. Recommend reducing non-coaching campaign sends by 40%.

2

Revive the Weekly Coaching Newsletter

The "Thursday Coaching Newsletter" averaged 20.6% OR when active (Jan-Jun 2025) but was largely discontinued after June. During that gap, the list saw its worst months (Jul: 12.9%, Oct-Nov: 15.4%). A consistent weekly coaching newsletter would provide a reliable engagement floor.

3

Address the List Decay Problem

The Coach list has lost 136K subscribers (-23.9%) since April 2025. At the current rate, the list will drop below 400K by mid-2026. Recommend: (1) run a re-engagement campaign for dormant subs, (2) implement a sunset policy for 90-day inactives, (3) add new Coach acquisition flows.

4

Optimize Subject Line Strategy

Top performers use curiosity gaps ("I followed every rule. But..."), identity triggers ("You're Meant to be a Life Coach"), and urgency ("your free pass is about to expire"). Bottom performers use income claims ("She made $10,000") and generic urgency ("[12 hours left]"). Avoid hype; lean into professional identity.

5

Reduce July-September Volume

The highest-volume months (Jul: 25 emails, Sep: 29 emails) had the worst open rates (12.9%, 16.1%). There's a clear inverse correlation between send volume and engagement. Cap Coach sends at 4/week maximum during non-launch periods.

6

CLC Is the Franchise — Double Down

CLC campaigns are the only ones that consistently break 20% OR on the Coach list across both years (2025: 21.7%, 2026: 21.0%). This audience was acquired for coaching certification — serve that intent. Pre-launch nurture sequences, alumni stories, and certification prep content would extend the CLC engagement window beyond launch weeks.
